How everything is calculated

Percentages

In all grouped results the percentage (%) column is calculated as the percent of a district's counts to the total counts of its group. For example in 'Frequencies by day', results are grouped by day, so each percentage for every district is calculated to the total for all districts on that specific day.

Because we are rounding off percentages to 2 decimal points, their sum across every group is very near but not exactly 100.

Decile

Deciles are calculated by discretizing count sums into 10 equal-length bins, sorted from lowest to highest. Each row is then assigned its bin number as a rank. Bin numbers start at 0. Since the range of each bin must be unique, any duplicate bins caused by duplicate values are dropped, so the maximum rank may be lower than 9. The values in dropped bins are redistributed to the remaining bins and so the bins may ultimately have unequal lengths.

Rank

Rank is calculated by scoring each row by its position in the sorted group. Scoring is done by a lowest-rank-is-best logic. For example if district X's position in the 'Monday' group in 'Frequencies by day' is in row 3, it means it is the third best district and it will have a rank of 3.

Curent time/day/month is displayed by default

The current time interval, week day and month are always displayed at the top of each list. If a time segment (interval, day or month) does not have any results, the immediately next segment is shown first, in logical order (eg. if Sunday has no results Monday is shown first).
